# Viability of Tokenised Residential Property in Australia

**Kavan Mehta** · UNSW Sydney · May 2026

A feasibility analysis of blockchain-enabled fractional ownership structures for Australian residential property. This paper proposes a derivative architecture in which the property owner retains full legal and beneficial title while token holders receive economic exposure through a financial contract, resolving the stamp duty liability that has prevented prior Australian tokenisation models from achieving commercial viability.

## Key Findings

- **$232 billion** total addressable market identified, of which **88% is seller-driven** (equity release, portfolio rebalancing, forced property divisions)
- **Derivative architecture** resolves stamp duty exposure that renders beneficial-ownership tokenisation models commercially unviable
- **Mortgaged property listings** enabled for the first time through LVR covenant framework with three-tier intervention protocol
- **Global precedent** from Singapore (Project Guardian), UK (Property Digital Assets Act 2025), and Dubai (DAMAC/Mantra pilot) validates institutional demand

## Structure

1. **Status Quo** — Housing affordability crisis, supply constraints, growth-yield trap, regulatory complexity, market illiquidity
2. **Case for Tokenisation** — Buyer profiles, seller profiles, use cases, total addressable market
3. **Global Landscape** — Singapore, UK, Dubai, Australia's Project Acacia
4. **Risks & Mitigations** — ASIC classification, stamp duty, counterparty risk, LVR breach, liquidity, failure conditions
5. **Proposed Operating Framework** — Derivative architecture, governance, valuation methodology, token economics
